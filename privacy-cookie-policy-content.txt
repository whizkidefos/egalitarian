The Egalitarian Association ("we", "us", or "our") is a Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO) registered in England. We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data responsibly.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal information when you use our website, volunteer with us, make donations, or otherwise interact with our organisation.


1. WHO WE ARE
-------------

The Egalitarian Association
Registered Charity Number: 1216794
Email: info@theegalitarianassociation.org
Address: 15 Aqueduct Way, Manchester, M30 0YU

For any questions about this policy or your personal data, please contact us using the details above.


2. INFORMATION WE COLLECT
-------------------------

We may collect the following types of personal information:

**Information you provide directly:**
- Name, email address, phone number, and postal address
- Volunteer application details (interests, availability, skills)
- Donation information (amount, payment method - we do not store card details)
- Newsletter subscription preferences
- Messages and enquiries you send us

**Information collected automatically:**
- IP address and browser type
- Pages visited and time spent on our website
- Referring website or source
- Device information


3. HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION
------------------------------

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

- To process volunteer applications and coordinate volunteering activities
- To process donations and send receipts
- To send newsletters and updates about our work (with your consent)
- To respond to your enquiries and provide support
- To improve our website and services
- To comply with legal obligations
- To prevent fraud and ensure security


4. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING
-----------------------------

We process your personal data based on:

- **Consent**: When you subscribe to our newsletter or submit a volunteer application
- **Contractual necessity**: When processing donations
- **Legitimate interests**: For improving our services and communicating about our charitable work
- **Legal obligation**: When required by law (e.g., Gift Aid records)


5. DATA SHARING
---------------

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your information with:

- Payment processors (PayPal, Stripe) to process donations securely
- Email service providers to send newsletters
- IT service providers who help maintain our website
- Regulatory bodies when required by law

All third parties are required to protect your data and use it only for specified purposes.


6. DATA RETENTION
-----------------

We retain your personal data only as long as necessary:

- Volunteer records: For the duration of your volunteering plus 3 years
- Donation records: 7 years (for Gift Aid and accounting purposes)
- Newsletter subscribers: Until you unsubscribe
- Website analytics: 26 months


7. YOUR RIGHTS
--------------

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

- **Access**: Request a copy of your personal data
- **Rectification**: Ask us to correct inaccurate data
- **Erasure**: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal requirements)
- **Restriction**: Ask us to limit how we use your data
- **Portability**: Receive your data in a portable format
- **Object**: Object to certain processing activities
- **Withdraw consent**: Withdraw consent at any time

To exercise these rights, contact us at info@theegalitarianassociation.org.


8. DATA SECURITY
----------------

We implement appropriate security measures to protect your personal data, including:

- Secure HTTPS encryption on our website
- Password-protected access to databases
- Regular security updates and monitoring
- Limited staff access to personal data


9. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS
--------------------------

Your data is primarily stored within the UK/EEA. If we transfer data outside this area, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place.


10. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY
--------------------------

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Significant changes will be communicated via our website or email.


11. COMPLAINTS
--------------

If you have concerns about how we handle your data, please contact us first. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

Website: https://ico.org.uk
Phone: 0303 123 1113

COOKIE POLICY
================================================================================

Last updated: 23 February 2026


1. WHAT ARE COOKIES?
--------------------

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly, remember your preferences, and provide analytics about how visitors use the site.


2. HOW WE USE COOKIES
---------------------

Our website uses the following types of cookies:

**Essential Cookies**
These are necessary for the website to function and cannot be disabled. They include:
- Session cookies to maintain your login state
- Security cookies to prevent fraud
- Cookie consent preferences

**Analytics Cookies**
With your consent, we use analytics cookies to understand how visitors interact with our website. This helps us improve our content and user experience.
- Google Analytics (if enabled)

**Functional Cookies**
These remember your preferences and choices:
- Language preferences
- Form data (to prevent re-entering information)


3. COOKIES WE USE
-----------------

| Cookie Name          | Purpose                              | Duration    | Type       |
|---------------------|--------------------------------------|-------------|------------|
| wordpress_logged_in | Maintains login session              | Session     | Essential  |
| wp_lang             | Stores language preference           | Session     | Functional |
| _ga                 | Google Analytics - visitor tracking  | 2 years     | Analytics  |
| _gid                | Google Analytics - session tracking  | 24 hours    | Analytics  |
| cookie_consent      | Remembers your cookie preferences    | 1 year      | Essential  |


4. MANAGING COOKIES
-------------------

You can control cookies through:

**Browser settings**: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. See your browser's help section for instructions.

**Our cookie banner**: When you first visit our site, you can choose which cookies to accept.

**Opt-out links**:
- Google Analytics: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

Note: Blocking essential cookies may affect website functionality.


5. THIRD-PARTY COOKIES
----------------------

Some cookies are set by third-party services we use:

- **Google Analytics**: For website analytics
- **YouTube**: If we embed videos (only when you play them)
- **Social media**: If you use share buttons

These third parties have their own privacy policies governing their use of cookies.


6. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY
-------------------------

We may update this Cookie Policy as our website evolves or regulations change. Please check back periodically.


7. CONTACT US
-------------

For questions about our use of cookies, contact:

The Egalitarian Association
Email: info@theegalitarianassociation.org
